What is hourly:self?

hourly:self is a service that collects your Qself data, presents it in a meaningful way and helps you achieve your objectives.

hourly:self's goal is to securely collect, keep and present all your Qself information in a centralised manner. With all the information in one location, as opposed to scattered over various apps and services, you will be able to cross-reference and analyse all your data holistically.

The first instance of hourly:self will prompt you for Qself data at specified intervals and will present it on your dashboard. The next instances will include a plugin system to import data from various Qself services and apps, new useful representations of your data and integration with social networks.

How does it work?

  1. Sign up for a secure account at hourly:self. The registration process is quick and easy and all your data is secure.
  2. Create a new record to hold a series of data.
    Ex. You create a 'Sleep' record to track the number of hours you sleep each day.
  3. Set up a timer to prompt for your data. Future versions will also import it from your favourite QSelf apps and services.
    Ex. You set your 'Sleep' record to prompt you at nine o'clock every morning on how many hours you slept the last night.
  4. At the preset time, hourly:self will prompt you for your data.
    Ex. At nine o'clock in the morning you get a promt asking you to introduce the hours you have slept.
  5. View your data on your dashboard. Cross-reference it with other data.
    Ex. You check the amount of hours you sleep daily and cross-reference them to the amount of coffee you consume.
  6. Set targets or goals for your records and work towards improving your life!
    Ex. You set a target in coffee consumption.
  7. Track your progress towards the targets you have set. Observe how these impact on other records.
    Ex. You track your lower coffee consumption and analyse the effect it has on your sleeping hours.

Frequently Asked Questions

What does QSelf mean?
We use the term QSelf to refer to the quantified self movement. When we talk about QSelf data we refer to information relative to your daily activities such as sport, physical activity, sleep, food intake, weight, emotional states, lifelogging, etc.
